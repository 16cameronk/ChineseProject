Story of Chinese New Year

The beginning of the Chinese New Year started with a beast called the Nian. Nian would eat villagers but the villagers would put food in front of their doors at the beginning of every year. It was believed that after the Nian ate the food they prepared, it wouldn't attack any more people. One day, a villager decided to stop Nian. He put red paper on his house and to place firecrackers because Nian doesn't like Red. When the New Year was about to come, the villagers would wear red clothes, hang red lanterns, and red spring scrolls on windows and doors. People also used firecrackers to frighten away the Nian. From then on, Nian never came to the village again.

What?

Lunar New Year is an important Chinese festival celebrated at the turn of the lunisolar Chinese calendar. It is also known as the Spring Festival.

When?

Celebrations traditionally run from the eve to the Lantern Festival on the 15th day of the first calendar month. The first day of the New Year falls on some day between 21 January and 20 February

What is the Monkey Year?

This Year is the year of the "Red" Fire Monkey. The Fire Monkey is the most active and aggressive of the 5 Monkeys (Metal, Fire, Water, Wood, and Earth). Naturally dominant, he automatically gravitates towards leadership roles and is competitive in whatever he is doing. The Monkey is the 9th animal in 12 zodiac signs.
